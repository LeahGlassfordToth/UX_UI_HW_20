Radio K Responsive Web Redesign

About the Project

Radio K is a student run and listener supported college radio station. Their website houses a live streaming feature, event information, schedule and more. In a team of three, we worked on a redesign to streamline the focus of the homepage to increase listening and, in turn, donations.

Challenge:

Music streaming services are dominating the scene. How can we appeal to those familiar with streaming interfaces and their features while staying true to real, live radio online?

My Role:
  • User research & analysis
  • Competitor analysis
  • Persona creation
  • Wireframes
  • UI Design & Protoyping
  • Interaction Design
  • Usability Testing

The Process

Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype, Test

I used the entire design thinking process during this redesign

  • Gather qualitative data via user interviews
  • Define the goals of the business
  • Focus on research results to include features important to users
  • Create paper prototypes
  • Digitize and test wireframes/prototypes
  • Iterate based on feedback and repeat testing

Problem Definition

Problem Statement

Through our research, we learned that users want more ways to engage with local music, and that they enjoy an easy to use, familiar, and aesthetically pleasing music streaming experience. How can we encourage streaming users who typically use paid subscriptions to switch to RadioK in order to increase website traffic and donations for the organization?

Research

User Surveys

Using Google Forms and Instagram, I put out two surveys to gather information on  use of paid streaming services and general listening habits of potential website visitors.

Results:

  • 71.4% said they most often listen to music via a paid streaming service
  • Only 17.9% surveyed were familiar with Radio K
  • 57% of participants prefer to listen to music radio
  • 17% prefer talk radio
  • 10% choose educational radio programming

User Interview Insights

We each conducted a user interview and together compiled our feedback into an Affinity Diagram.

Results:

  • Users love ad-free streaming services
  • They want supporting local music to be simpler
  • They have habits and set expectations for music streaming

User Flow

Ideation

Initial Sketches

Before solidifying our design direction, we discussed the essential elements to implement and split up to each sketch a version of our new hompage and music streaming modal.

Findings:

  • To focus on streaming, we’d start with a landing page to stream live or continue
  • We’d like to give users the ability to stream while viewing the site
  • We have a good base website to start with and build upon

Wireframes

We agreed that Radio K had a recognizable brand with good use of color so without changing everything, our main goal was to upgrade the information architecture and create a visual hierarchy. We wanted to emphasize certain elements while keeping a bulleting board style grid inspired a college campus student union. We gave each element more space to breathe and condensed navigation options to streamline the homepage.

Testing

User tests on our wireframes revealed page inconsistencies, incorrect links, and an unsuccessful donate flow. Users weren’t able to infer that to complete the donation form, they needed to scroll down on the page for a final button. We made sure all pages clicked to the correct links, elements from page to page would be in the same spot, and added a scroll bar indication on the donation page as well as filling out content and images for the flows we selected to protoype.

Conclusion

What I've learned

  • User research is key - Although we would have loved to have gotten input from the organization, user feedback consistently proves the most valuable. Getting a fresh set of eyes to point out what doesn't make sense or what is going well is absolutely essential.
  • Iterate...again - Getting feedback was a practice in maintaining project scope. We chose to focus on increasing streaming and implementing a landing page. As we did testing and got feedback at different stages, it was a challenge to maintain what was achievable and loop back to our original business & user focus.
  • Design isn't development - Clearly, development of prototypes is a totally different category from clickable mock-ups, but it was interesting to learn what may or may not actually be possible from our initial ideas. Having very little understanding of backend development, we created a streaming modal and artists' about pages that may not actually be able to be implemented but were interesting ideas to explore regardless.
